About Jaunpur

Jaunpur, a part of the Varanasi division of Uttar Pradesh, is known for its rich culture and historical significance. Well-known for its past and the glory of learning Jaunpur holds its own important historical, social and political status. The glory of the city on the Adi Ganga Gomti and its peaceful shores was a major pious ground for the meditations and contemplations of sages, Rishis and Maharshis from where the sounds of the vedmantras emanated. In 14th century by Feroze Shah Tughlaq named it Jaunpur in memory of his cousin Muhammad Bin Tughlaq, fondly known as Jauna Khan. Over the years, it was conquered and ruled by many regimes including the Sharqi dynasty and the Mughals.

Jaunpur is situated at the center of many historical and religious places of Bharat. The famous Shakti Peeth, Goddess Shitla Maa (Chaukiya Dham) is just 8km from the campus. Ayodhya, the birth place of Shri Ram is 130 km from the university campus while the ancient city, Kashi (Varanasi) is 80 km away. The Sangam city, Prayagraj is 110 km from the university campus. Goddess Vindhyavasini temple (Vindhyachal Dham) is situated in district Mirzapur is located 90Km away. All these places are well connected by road, rail and air.
